Project 1

AI Workflows for Research and Presentations

A three-session course for teenagers on using AI to move from a good source, to a clear summary, to a presentation outline.

Audience: ages 16–18 Format: 3 × 1 hour Focus: research → summary → slides

Project goal

Teach a practical AI workflow

Students learn how to go from trusted source material to structured output, while keeping human review in the loop.

Session map

1. Good Input, Good Output 2. Build a Simple AI Workflow 3. From Summary to Presentation

Project summary

Teach AI as a tool, not a substitute for judgment.

This project introduces students to a responsible workflow for using AI. The goal is not blind trust. The goal is to teach students how to start with a credible source, structure a task clearly, generate a first draft, and then improve it with human review.

The full project is divided into three sessions so students can learn the idea step by step.

Sessions

Three sections

Session 1

Good Input, Good Output

Source quality

Students learn that AI is only as useful as the source and the task they provide. The session introduces credibility, evidence, and the idea of garbage in, garbage out.

  • Identify trustworthy vs weak sources
  • Learn a simple 5-point trust framework
  • Compare weak prompts with stronger structured prompts
Session 2

Build a Simple AI Workflow

Repeatable process

Students move from one-off prompting to workflow thinking. The emphasis is on repeatability: input an article, generate a structured summary, and convert it into something reusable.

  • Understand the difference between a prompt and a workflow
  • Build or modify a no-code AI flow
  • Return a structured summary with key findings and limitations
Session 3

From Summary to Presentation

Communication

Students turn research output into a simple presentation outline. They also learn that AI output still needs editing, critique, and better structure before it becomes useful communication.

  • Create a 5-slide outline from a report or article
  • Critique weak AI-generated slide structures
  • Explain where human judgment still matters most

Deliverables

What students should leave with

A trusted source, a structured AI summary, a 5-slide outline, and a short reflection on what AI did well and what still required human judgment.

Core message

AI helps accelerate useful work

The real skill is not just using AI. It is choosing good input, structuring the task clearly, reviewing the output, and turning information into something usable.
